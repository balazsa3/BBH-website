Artist & Goldsmith

Brigitta B. Horvath is a contemporary artist balancing fine art and applied art. She is known for her unique approach to metalworking, combining traditional techniques with modern innovations. Her artistic vision is characterized by an organic, metaphysical, and minimalist approach.

Brigitta B. Horváth is a member of the generation of metalworking object designers who bridge traditional goldsmithing and object design in contemporary applied art. She possesses two perspectives and technical skills: she knows and applies traditional craft techniques while also adapting to and using modern technological innovations. Few have remained in this field from the so-called transitional generation, as the modernist approach in traditional metalworking and the traditionalist approach in contemporary design do not fit into the market-based categories of object creation and applied art. Therefore, it is necessary to support creators who consider the design and execution of everyday objects as an expression of art.

Marton Keppel

Art Historian

Brigitta B. Horváth's artistic vision is characterized by the keywords: organic, metaphysical, and minimalist. She realizes these qualities in her individually crafted objects using goldsmithing techniques. She is one of the few designers who bravely engages in copperwork even when artistic copperworking is disappearing among the genres of contemporary goldsmithing. She found her unique voice through the creation of standing and hanging light fixtures. By reflecting light and metal on each other, she creates an atmosphere in the space that highlights the object from its functional environment and forms a kind of zen aura around the user.

Marton Keppel

Art Historian

As a gallery, the Prague RESONANCE Gallery was founded by Associate Professor Petr Vogel, who taught at the metal and jewelry studio of the Academy of Fine Arts in Prague... In each parallel long showcase, the series of jewelry works ranged from world stars to domestic stars to beginners, forming a kind of condensed essence. In one of them, the works of the aforementioned Prague RESONANCE Gallery shared space with the materials of the Szentendre MANA Jewelry Studio, a training place led by Katalin Jermakov, a Ferenczy Award-winning jewelry designer who has been holding international jewelry design workshops for 20 years. Here we saw works from 15 Hungarian students, ranging from professional designers to enthusiastic hobby goldsmiths. From here, I would highlight the spectacular yet deeply symbolic brooches formed from hands by Brigitta B. Horváth, titled Hope and Love you more. I note here that besides the aesthetics of the objects, it is also an artistic symptom that the jewelry in this artistic segment has titles.
